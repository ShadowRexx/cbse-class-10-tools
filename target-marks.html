How to Use This Target Calculator

This calculator helps you plan your study goals by showing exactly how many marks you need to achieve your desired CBSE Class 10 percentage. It's based on the official CBSE Best of 5 calculation method.

📊 Calculation Formula:

Required Marks Per Subject = (Target Percentage × 500) ÷ 100 ÷ 5

Where 500 = Maximum total marks (5 subjects × 100 marks each)

Understanding Your Results

  • Average Marks: This is the score you need in each of your best 5 subjects
  • Best of 5: CBSE picks your highest-scoring 5 subjects out of 6
  • Flexibility: You can score higher in some subjects and lower in others, as long as the average matches
  • Language Rule: One language subject (English/Hindi) must be included in your best 5

Smart Study Tips

🎯 Set Realistic Goals

Choose a target percentage that challenges you but is achievable based on your current performance.

📊 Balance Your Subjects

Focus extra effort on subjects where you can improve the most. Some subjects may need more attention than others.

📝 Track Your Progress

Use pre-board exams and practice tests to monitor if you're on track to meet your target marks.

🚀 Use Your 6th Subject

Your 6th subject is a safety net. If you perform better in it than another subject, it will count instead.

Common Target Percentages

  • 95-100%: Requires 95-100 marks per subject - Excellent performance needed
  • 90-95%: Requires 90-95 marks per subject - Very good performance needed
  • 85-90%: Requires 85-90 marks per subject - Good, achievable target
  • 75-85%: Requires 75-85 marks per subject - Moderate effort required
  • 60-75%: Requires 60-75 marks per subject - First class performance

Important Notes

  • ✅ The calculator shows the average marks needed - you can vary across subjects
  • ✅ Your actual marks can be distributed differently (e.g., 95, 92, 88, 85, 90 averages to 90)
  • ✅ Focus on your stronger subjects while maintaining passing marks in all subjects
  • ✅ Remember that passing all subjects is mandatory, regardless of your percentage goal
  • ✅ This is a planning tool - consistent study and practice are key to success
