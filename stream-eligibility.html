CBSE Class 10 Stream Selection Guide (2025)

Choosing the right stream after Class 10 is one of the most important academic decisions for CBSE students. Your stream determines which subjects you will study in Class 11 and 12 and directly affects your future career options.

Most schools offer three main streams after CBSE Class 10: Science, Commerce, and Arts (Humanities). Eligibility for each stream depends on your percentage and subject-wise performance.

Science Stream Eligibility

Science is usually the most competitive stream. Many schools require 75%‚Äì85% overall percentage for admission into Science. For PCM (Physics, Chemistry, Maths), strong marks in Mathematics are important. For PCB (Physics, Chemistry, Biology), good marks in Science are required.

  • Recommended Percentage: 75% and above
  • Strong performance in Maths & Science
  • Ideal for Engineering, Medical, Research, NDA, and Technical careers

Commerce Stream Eligibility

Commerce is suitable for students interested in business, finance, CA, CS, MBA, banking, and entrepreneurship. Schools generally require 60%‚Äì75% overall percentage for Commerce. Mathematics may be required in some schools for Commerce with Maths.

  • Recommended Percentage: 60% and above
  • Good analytical and numerical skills helpful
  • Ideal for CA, Business, Finance, Management

Arts / Humanities Stream Eligibility

Arts is a flexible stream with diverse career options including Law, Civil Services, Journalism, Psychology, Design, and Government jobs. Schools usually allow admission with 50%‚Äì60% percentage or above.

  • Recommended Percentage: 50% and above
  • Strong writing and theoretical understanding helpful
  • Ideal for UPSC, Law, Media, Teaching, Social Sciences

Important Things to Remember

  • ‚úî Some schools prioritize subject-wise marks
  • ‚úî Your interest matters more than just percentage
  • ‚úî Stream selection does not permanently limit your future options
